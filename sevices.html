REAL MADRID

Real Madrid Club de Fútbol, commonly known as Real Madrid or simply Real, is a Spanish professional football club based in Madrid, Spain. Founded on 6 March 1902 as Madrid Football Club, the club was granted the title "Real" (Royal) by King Alfonso XIII in 1920, which led to its current name. The club has traditionally worn a white home kit since its inception, a choice inspired by the English team Corinthian, and is nicknamed Los Blancos (The Whites). It also holds other nicknames such as Los Merengues (The Meringues), Los Vikingos (The Vikings), and La Casa Blanca (The White House).

BARCELONA

FC Barcelona is one of the most iconic football clubs in the world, known for its rich history, legendary players, and unique cultural identity. It represents more than just sport—it’s a symbol of Catalan pride and global excellence.

PARIS-SAINT-GERMAIN

sParis Saint-Germain Football Club, commonly referred to as Paris Saint-Germain (PSG), is a French professional football club based in Paris, founded in 1970 following the merger of Paris FC and Stade Saint-Germain. The club competes in Ligue 1, the top division of French football, and has played 55 seasons, with 52 of them in the top flight since their promotion in 1974. They are the most successful French club in history, with a total of 56 official titles, including a record 13 Ligue 1 championships, 16 Coupe de France, nine Coupe de la Ligue, and 13 Trophée des Champions. They also hold the record for the most consecutive seasons in Ligue 1, having competed in the top division for 51 seasons since 1974–75.
